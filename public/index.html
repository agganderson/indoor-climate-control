Our Commissioning Philosophy

The Commissioning Agent should be a highly experienced individual with no conflicts of interest. The firm should possess no licenses or certifications which would allow the Agent to be in competition with the Project Team members. The Firm should not rely on anyone but the Owner or Owner's Representative for fees or income.

The Commissioning (Cx) Process

The Owner's Project Requirements (OPR)

We provide a template and assist the Owner in expressing what they expect out of their building, including indoor environmental details and specific environmental considerations for the site. This is a "wish list" for the owner.

The Basis of Design (BoD)

The Design Team or Design/Build Contractor generates a document which describes the details of the building and systems which are proposed to satisfy the OPR. This is often impacted by the budget, leading to a revised OPR. We review the BoD and comment on it's impact on the OPR.

Pre-Functional Checklists (PFC)

We provide checklists to the Contractors for them to verify that their installation meets the Specifications (if they exist) and industry standard practices. We perform field visits to confirm the installation and add items to the Issues Log.

The Issues Log

Starting with the BoD and continuing through the end of the Cx Process, we start and maintain a og of all the Issues that are generated during the Process. This provides the Owner with a clear, simple method of tracking changes and corrections and is a valuable tool for documenting compromises and installation realities that occur during construction.

Functional Performance Testing (FPT)

This is where the rubber meets the road. Based on our review of the submittals and the Design, we generate a sequence of testing procedures for all the Commissioned Systems. All FPT occurs after the installations are up and running and thoroughly tested by the installing Contractors. We witness the FPT and document any deviations on the Issues Log.

The Final Cx Report

All the documentation generated during the Cx Process is compiled in an organized document allowing the Owner verify the correct installation and operation of all the Cx Systems and provides for a method of regularly scheduled verification of correct operation.
